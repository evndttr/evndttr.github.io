Interactive Maps and Timelines

At Princeton University Library, I built public-facing digital exhibits for a variety of collaborative library-based research projects. I served as a research assistant and consultant on an NEH-funded international collaboration focused on digitally repatriating an 18th-century Spanish colonial archive, a role which involved coordinating between researchers and institutional partners and building web content. The frames below demonstrate some prototype maps and timelines built using open-source tools developed by Knightlab at Northwestern University, StoryMapJS and TimelineJS, for a NEH-funded inter-institutional digital humanities project affiliated with Princeton University's Spanish and Portuguese department.
